Problem Statement: Time Management App for Computer Science Students

Computer science students at universities often face challenges in time management when dealing with intensive programming projects and theoretical assignments. They need a time management app that helps plan both short-term and long-term academic tasks, and provides custom reminders and progress tracking. This app should be simple to use, seamlessly integrating with their daily study routines, thus helping them improve learning efficiency, reduce procrastination, and complete academic tasks with high quality within strict deadlines.

Team Personas: Alex Johnson

Alex Johnson, a 22-year-old Senior College Student and Part-time Software Development Intern from Austin, Texas, embraces the philosophy to "Stay hungry, stay foolish." - Steve Jobs. He balances a busy schedule by going for a run every morning and learning new programming languages in his spare moments. His comfort with technology spans from the internet to mobile apps, and he values innovation, efficiency, and continuous learning. Alex needs an Integrated Development Environment (IDE) with high contrast and code syntax highlighting. He wants his academic and work projects to sync automatically to a personal digital calendar. Despite his tech-savviness, Alex fears security issues due to code vulnerabilities and struggles to keep up with rapid technological changes.
